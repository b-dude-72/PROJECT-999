Case Study

A case of type 2 diabetes mellitus patient with cardiovascular comorbidities

CASE HISTORY

Businessman came to me as per his routine follow-up every 3 months and had 8-year history of type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM), hypertension and dyslipidemia.

Patient complained of hunger pangs, occasional dizziness, sweating and weakness since past one week, especially before lunch time. He also kept a record for weight for his routine visit, and he noted a weight gain of 4 kgs since a month. He also mentioned swelling in his legs, and intermittent presentation of infection in urinary tract (UTI). Incidence of hypoglycemia was occasionally reported. He also mentioned his daily routine to be erratic in terms of diet and exercise since a year due to frequent business visits and outings.

What were the patient's family history and medication history?

The patient's father had a history of Ischemic heart disease and T2DM. At the time of diagnosis, this patient had HbA1c of 9.3%, and was treated with metformin 1g twice a day, aspirin 75 mg /day, telmisartan 40 mg/day and atorvastatin 10 mg /day. After 9 months of initial treatment, this patient showed HbA1c of 8.2% and his treatment regimen was revised to glimepiride 1 mg/day and metformin 500 mg thrice daily. His blood pressure and lipid parameters also improved significantly. His hypertension treatment regimen and dyslipidemia treatment were continued as before. Further, he had continuous follow-up regularly every 3 months and his clinical parameters were under control, until current visit.

What did his current physical examination and
clinical investigations reveal?
Weak and tired
Body mass index: 28 kg/m2
Body pressure: 150/98 mmHg
Fasting bloog glucose: 154 mg/dL
Postprandial bloog glucose: 204 mg/dL
Low-density lipoprotein cholestrol: 178 mg/dL
HbA1c: 7.9%

Did the patient showed any concern for ongoing
treatment regimen?

The patient's fasting blood glucose and HbAlc was still above the normal range. Thus, he was considered for the change in his treatment regimen. However, he had some concerns. He mentioned, due to his busy lifestyle and office routine, he was concerned of weight gain and was worried of symptoms like palpitations, dizziness, poor concentration and sweating. He approached for a treatment regimen that would not impact his daily-life activities.

What was the treatment considered in this patient?

Based on the clinical findings, the patient was considered for treatment change for adequate glycemic control. Since this patient was concerned about the weight gain, incidence of hypoglycemia, treatment with glimepiride was stopped and an alternative therapy was considered. Thus, treatment with vildagliptin-metformin combination 50mg-1g twice daily was initiated. Also, telmisartan/HCTZ in doses of 40 mg/12.5 mg once daily and rosuvastatin 20 mg/day were administered for the treatment of hypertension and dyslipidemia, respectively. In addition to these, this patient was also strictly advised for lifestyle modification measures and physical activity.

What were the lifestyle approaches recommended for this patient?

Patient was advised for low carbohydrate and high protein diet Restricted intake of fatty foods and salt was also advised. Intake of fiber-rich foods like beans, okra, oatmeal was advised. Physical activities included aerobic exercises like walking, cycling, jogging, resistance exercises like free-weight and balance exercises like yoga. Physical activities for at least 30 to 60 minutes per day.

How did the patient respond to the prescribed treatment?

After 3 months, his clinical investigation findings were as follows:

The patient showed significant improvement in his glycemic and renal parameters, and metformin- vildagliptin combination was well tolerated with no complaints of hypoglycemia. He reported weight loss of 2 kgs after 3 months, and was advised to continue metformin-vildagliptin combination treatment.

Did the patient follow lifestyle modification advice regularly?

Considering his schedule at work, the patient had faced difficulty to do lifestyle changes and maintain his work activities together. However, with determination, great efforts and realization of impairment in his health, he had followed my treatment advice and used to perform jogging, cycling, yoga and meditation for at least 20 minutes a day. He also followed the diet-related changes during the treatment. Thus, he was found be fit and strong during the follow-up visit. He did not report any incidence of hypoglycemia

Why did i share this case?

Despite his age and lot of co-morbidities, overall this patient responded well to the treatment and his glycemic and lipid parameters were under control with all his efforts by maintaining lifestyle changes and following adequate pharmacological treatment that suited him. While almost any medication would be effective in this patient, not all would be equally durable, well tolerated, safe or hassle free in his case
