We have to choose what is right and that too on time



Harsha Rao
47 Years

Hi, I am Harsha Rao, an investment banker. I was diagnosed with Type 2 Diabetes in 2014 after I started feeling low in energy, excessively thirsty and had frequent urination. I was told to do an HbA1c test at that time and the results were shocking. My HbA1c was 9 ; looking at it doctor clearly said that I am have type 2 diabetes. He then gave me some meds to deal with the sugar.

I just loved to eat "Junk"!
I didn't feel any sense of earnestness to change my lifestyle considering that it's a family issue; since, my mother had it, so I just thought- "I had to deal with it". I was overweight, had a sedentary life and had uncontrolled urge to eat whatever is unhealthy. I believed that medicines would take care of the diabetes and since, I had no other troubling symptoms that alarmed me, I didn't have enough motivation to change my habits.

Then came the warning from my body!

But 2 years back when I felt that something was wrong with my eyesight, the reality hit me right between my eyes. Along with the altered vision, I was exhausted, not able to sleep, had dry mouth, and had a strong feeling that that something was not right.

I then went to see my doctor and found out that my blood sugar was totally out of control (A1C was around 13). Doctor had increased my existing medicines and added some new ones too!.

Time to cut the unhealthy carbs for cure and care!
Then, on doctor's recommendation, I decided I would take complete control and would not let diabetes take my life away from my family and the effect it had on my family's life. For next six months, I completely changed diet of myself and my family, began to exercise, and learned to "eat healthy".

From Fat to Fit
At the 7th month, I have lost 7 kgs, by A1c was 6.2 and doctor has cut down on my medications also and stopped prescribing cholesterol controlling medicines which he had previously prescribed. All I would say is "we have to choose what is right and that too on time". There's no in between. Loving yourself is being healthy for yourself and your family too.
