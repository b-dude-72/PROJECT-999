Living with diabetes since 2 decades



Ashwin Tripathi
60 Years

Hi everyone. I am Ashwin Tripathi. I was diagnosed with diabetes in 20 years ago when I was 40. So, you can say that I lived with Type 2 diabetes for major chunk of my life.In these 20 years I have seen many changes, some of which I only vaguely remember.

When I came to know…
I still remember being diagnosed, after I developed a mouth thrush and a fungal infection, symptoms that are not at all typical of diabetes. My family doctor was bit slow picking up on it. After 2 visits, the blood test was done and diabetes came into my life. I was suggested to follow a diabetes friendly lifestyle; like healthy low-in-added sugar healthy diet and a regular 30 minutes of exercise every day. Along with that doctor had started with my meds too which I am now used to taking it regularly without fail.

Now I am 60…

Twenty years later with healthy food habits, regular exercise and medication routine, regular follow-ups with doctor and regular blood sugar and A1c check-ups, I am doing just fine, thanks to all the changes that I implemented positively after being diagnosed with diabetes!

My dietician helps me to choose good carbs over bad ones
On my doctor’s recommendation, I have been planning meals with my dietician who helps me choose good and complex carbohydrate diet over white carbs and keep a count on food and calorie intake to keep the sugar levels under control.

Like I have now totally switched to homemade Indian breads made of whole grains like wheat, bajra, ragi and various other millets which do not shoot up the blood sugar levels after eating. I have stopped eating bad carbs which include white bread, white rice or anything made out of maida or refined flour

One thing I learned from my 20 year journey is that by doing modest changes in your food habits, lifestyle, exercise routine and by maintaining a healthy weight, one can easily lead a healthy and joyous life without even being felt like you have Diabetes!

My small message to all the readers out there…. do not fear diabetes! Small things and modest control can help a lot to live healthily and happily even if one has diabetes for a long time.
