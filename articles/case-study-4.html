Taking first step is important



Dilip Singh
48 Years

Hi everyone I am Dilip, I am 48-year-old. I was first diagnosed with diabetes a decade ago. I had been controlling it for many years with diet and exercise. Then before 4 years or so, I developed a skin rash and my doctor prescribed a medication after which increased my glucose levels over 435 mg/dL. I was immediately placed on insulin and frequent testing and insulin injections turned my world upside down.

Sugar was controlled but only for a while
After insulin was initiated, my sugar was controlled and came to around 176 mg/dL. I tried to follow limited carbohydrate diet for sometime but found it very difficult to stick to it. I also went on frequent business outings at that time for a rough duration of 5-6 months. On regular check-up and follow-up with the doctor, I came to know that my glucose levels had again hiked to 378mg/dL. My doctor now referred me for diabetes education, along with insulin.

Diabetes Education changed my way of living

Through proper diabetes education, I learned how to control my glucose levels, treating and preventing the highs and lows of diabetes; what are the medications and what do they do.I also learned to measure glucose levels on a daily basis; counting carbs and controlled portion size of meals for better glucose control; and the importance of daily exercise for controlling blood glucose levels. My diabetes educator told me what to eat, what not to eat, created diet plans of me and encouraged me to eat foods that do not increase your blood sugar levels to a huge extent (low glycemic index).

My doctor played a huge part in my success

She motivated me in every way possible and encouraged me to live well. Today, I am no longer taking insulin. I am on my usual oral medicines and try to control my diabetes along with diet and exercise. I have kicked out sweets like candy and cookies for good and measuring carbohydrates and portion size are in my daily routine. My exercise includes 30 mins of walking each day.

My glucose levels are consistent, ranging from 90-130 mg/dl (and HbA1c of 7.5) on a regular basis.

Its never too late to start

My doctor said that age is a risk factor for type 2 diabetes, a large percentage of individuals are diagnosed in their 60's, 70's, and 80's. But my personal experience says that making changes is important; no matter if you are 40 years old or 80 years old.Its never too late to start. Taking first step is important

My doctor and diabetes educator helped me get a handle on my diabetes and I now have peace of mind.
